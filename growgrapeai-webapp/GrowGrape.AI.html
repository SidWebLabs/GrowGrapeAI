Nutrition & Growth Management

Pruning (October and November)

Timing: October-November preparation

Purpose: Remove old wood, shape structure

Methods: Cane/spur pruning techniques

Bud Break and Shoot Growth (November and December)

Timing: November-December growth period

Development: New shoot emergence

Care: Irrigation and pest monitoring

Flowering (December and January)

Timing: December-January

Development: Flowering begins

Care: Ensure optimal vine health for pollination

Fruit Set and Berry Development (December, January, and February)

Timing: Fruit set after flowering, berry growth from December to February

Development: Pollinated flowers develop into berries

Care: Manage irrigation and fertilizers, monitor for diseases

Ripening (January, February, and March)

Timing: January-March

Development: Berries reach full color, sweetness, and flavor

Care: Control irrigation to avoid over-watering, monitor pests and diseases

Harvesting (February to April)

Timing: February-April, depending on variety

Method: Hand-picking to minimize damage

Post-Harvest: Cool grapes, sort, and pack based on market requirements

Kharad Chaatani (April)

Timing: April

Purpose: Rejuvenate vines, prepare for the next season

Method: Pruning, applying organic matter, and other vineyard practices

Shoot and Leaf Development (May to July)

Timing: May-July

Development: Shoot elongation and leaf expansion

Care: Maximize photosynthesis, support future fruiting

Bud Differentiation (July to August)

Timing: July-August

Development: Fruit bud differentiation

Purpose: Critical stage for determining next year's fruit quality

Carbohydrate Storage (August to September)

Timing: August-September

Development: Storage of carbohydrates in roots and trunk

Purpose: Energy reserves for future growth

Preparation for Dormancy (October)

Timing: October

Development: Vine preparation for dormancy

Purpose: Ensure survival through colder months
